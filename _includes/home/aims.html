Project aims.

our aims

Protecting and preserving our historic cinema

A refurbishment of our main auditorium will restore key heritage features like our original gas lighting, while behind the scenes we’ll be making sure we can continue to project 35mm film. We’ll also be expanding our role as a heritage organisation, doing more to promote the rich history of film in Yorkshire.

our aims

A building that’s open to everyone

A key motivation for making changes to our building is the need to make sure it’s a space that’s accessible by all. We’ll be implementing step-free access to all parts of the building (including accessible toilets), but we’ll also be working hard to ensure that we’re a safe, welcoming and comfortable space for other groups like those with autism or dementia.

our aims

Creating the best film watching experience in Leeds

Whether you like watching the latest oscar winners, foreign language independents, or gems from the archive, a second screen will mean a programme with twice as many films. That means we’ll be able to better cater for everyone. A refurbishment of our main auditorium will also mean that we’re able to show the latest films using the latest technology, so you’re always seeing film the way it’s meant to be seen.

our aims

A picture house that works for its community

We’ve been working with and for the local community for many years. With a wider range of multipurpose spaces and longer opening hours, we’ll become a community asset that local people, groups and schools can use for film and other events. We’ll also be developing our role as a place of education – supporting young filmmakers throughout local communities.
